## Top 3 discoveries (the most important findings)

### 1. **bitFlyer's bFFX (FX_BTC_JPY) perp has a self-arbitrageable SFD mechanism** (basis capped ±5% to spot)

The bitFlyer retail-Arb community (note.com, diamond.jp) explicitly documents the FX-vs-spot basis as a 0.5%-per-round-trip edge when deviation > 4%. The edge is **structurally capped by the SFD mechanism** (designed to prevent the edge from being too large). With $50K notional, $250/round-trip × 4-6 trades/week = $4-6K/month ($40-72K annualized) — meaningful but small for $10K book.

The **critical insight** is that the SFD mechanism is designed to LIMIT the edge, but the FX spot volume ($76-122M daily) is too small to support >$50K positions. This is a capital-constrained edge, not a latency-constrained one — Tokyo co-loc adds zero incremental edge.

**Source:** https://note.com/ycrypthon/n/nad992be6fb11 (Japanese Python arb bot tutorial), https://bitflyer.com/pub/20241028-explanation-crypto-asset-regulation-amendment-ja.pdf (SFD official documentation)

### 2. **Hyperliquid validators in AWS ap-northeast-1 (Tokyo) — 2-3ms RTT from Tokyo colo**

This is the ONLY major venue where Tokyo co-loc gives a meaningful sub-3ms RTT to the matching engine. Hyperliquid is a new venue (launched 2023), grew to 80% of DEX perp market by 2025-08, 13.6% of Binance volume. Retail fee: 0.045% taker / 0.015% maker (cheapest). Top-tier maker rebate: -0.003% (paid to trade, requires $500M+ 14d volume).

**But:** Hyperliquid is on-chain (Arbitrum), so "RTT" is a soft signal — orders go through mempool then settle on L1. The latency edge is for **cross-venue arb (Hyperliquid vs Binance/Bybit)**, not intra-Hyperliquid. For mm-crypto-bot Phase 14E scope, this is irrelevant unless the project adds Hyperliquid as a venue — which would require a $1M+ book to clear the volume needed to extract the maker rebate.

**Source:** https://www.binance.com/en/square/post/307123585625377 (Hyperliquid validator AWS Tokyo), https://hyperliquidkorea.com/trading-fees (fee schedule), https://tianpan.co/investment-memo/2025-10-16-the-1011-crypto-storm (1011 absorption detail)

### 3. **Bybit's matching engine is in AWS Singapore, NOT Tokyo — Tokyo colo is BACKWARDS for Bybit**

The widespread assumption "Asian exchange → Tokyo colo" is wrong for Bybit. Arbitron's empirical latency measurements: Bybit responds in 16ms from Singapore, 91ms from Tokyo. **A retail co-loc in Tokyo loses 75ms to a retail co-loc in Singapore for the same venue.**

This is a critical finding because:
- The mm-crypto-bot Phase 14E primary venue is bybit.eu
- bybit.eu matching is also NOT in Tokyo (likely AWS eu-central-1 / Vienna)
- Tokyo colo for bybit.eu = 200-300ms RTT (worse than any other location)
- Singapore colo for bybit.eu = 180-250ms (similar; marginal improvement)
- **For bybit.eu specifically, NO colo location gives <1ms RTT**

Tokyo colo only makes sense for: (a) Hyperliquid (2-3ms — verified), (b) bitFlyer (sub-1ms — own datacenters), (c) Binance USDT-M (10-15ms — secondary Asia region). For the Phase 14E bybit.eu mandate, Tokyo colo is the wrong choice.

**Source:** https://arbitron.app/learn/bybit-server-location (Singapore primary), https://nikhilpadala.com/blog/exchange-co-location-cloud/ (Bybit Equinix TY11 is backup, not primary)
